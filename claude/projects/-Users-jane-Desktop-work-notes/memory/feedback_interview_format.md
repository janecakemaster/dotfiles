---
name: Interview feedback formatting
description: Structural and formatting rules for behavioral interview feedback files — bullet style, section brevity, recommendation conciseness
type: feedback
---

Use `*` bullet points with blank lines between each bullet within a section — not prose paragraphs.

**Why:** Jane reformatted dense paragraph-style feedback into separated bullet points for readability in Ashby.

**How to apply:**
- Each evidence point is its own `*` bullet with a blank line before it
- Section intro (one line of context like role/tenure) stands alone before the bullets
- Keep the overall recommendation section concise — state the concern in 1-2 sentences, don't re-elaborate on gaps already covered in individual sections
- Don't hedge or soften in the recommendation (no "not a clear no" type language)
- Don't explain what our team needs or what TC would need to ramp on — just state the gap
- Include a section for every interview question asked — map each question to its focus attribute (e.g., "Growth Mindset & Continuous Learning"). Don't skip questions even if the answer overlaps with another section.
